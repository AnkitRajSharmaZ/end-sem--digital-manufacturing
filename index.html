1. Pre-Processing:

  • Software: LOMSlice‚Ñ¢.
  • Generates image from CAD (STL).
  • Functions: Slicing, Orienting, Merging.

2. Building (The Cycle):

  • Bonding: Platform rises. A heated roller (reciprocal motion) bonds new material to the stack. Temperature control is critical for uniform lamination.

  • Decubing: Removing the block and separating the part from the cross-hatched support cubes using wood carving tools.

‚öôÔ∏è Detailed Process

1. The Head (Liquifier):

  • Uses a Dual Tip Mechanism: One nozzle for Model material, one for Support material.
  • Heats filament to semi-liquid state (0.5¬∞C above melting point).
  • Floating elements inside liquifier ensure smooth flow.

2. Deposition:

  • Material bonds thermally to the previous layer.
  • Surrounding air is kept just below melting point to allow instant solidification.

3. PolyJet Printing

Core Tech: Inkjet heads jetting Liquid Photopolymers + UV Curing.

üîß Tech Specs

  • Layer Resolution: 16 microns (0.0006"). Ultra-fine!
  • Accuracy: 0.1 - 0.3 mm.
  • Min Wall Thickness: 1 mm.
  • Hardness Range: Shore 27A (Rubber) to 95A (Rigid).

‚öôÔ∏è Process Steps

  1. Pre-Processing: Software calculates model and support placement.
  2. Production:
    • Carriage moves X-Y. Jets droplets.
    • UV Lamps on carriage cure droplets immediately.
    • Build tray lowers (Z-axis).
    • Multi-jet heads allow Simultaneous Jetting of model AND support.
  3. Support Removal: Gel-like support removed by water jet or hand.

üåü "Digital Materials":

PolyJet can mix resins on the fly. It can create gradients (rigid to flexible) and mix colors. It simulates overmolds (e.g., a toothbrush handle with rubber grip) in ONE PRINT.

‚úÖ Strengths

  • Aesthetics: Smooth, glossy finish.
  • Multi-Material: 1000s of material options via mixing.

‚ùå Weaknesses

  • Cost: Resins are very expensive.
  • Durability: Photopolymers degrade in UV light/Heat over time.
  • Support Marks: Matte finish where support touches, Glossy where it doesn't.

4. LENS (Laser Engineered Net Shaping)

Company: Optomec (Albuquerque, NM).
Tech Family: Directed Energy Deposition (DED).

üîß Machine: Model 850-R

  • Laser: Nd:YAG or Fiber Laser (500 W to 2 kW).
  • Build Volume: 900 x 1500 x 900 mm (Huge!).
  • Components: Argon Recirculation Unit, Glove Box, Hermetic Chamber (Oxygen-free).

‚öôÔ∏è Process Physics

  1. Molten Puddle: Laser focuses on metal substrate, creating a melt pool.
  2. Powder Injection: Nozzles inject metal powder into the focal point/puddle.
  3. Fusion: Powder melts and bonds. Head moves to draw lines.
  4. Cooling: Rapid solidification results in fine grain structure (Better properties!).

5. General Digital Manufacturing Topics

A. Engineering Polymers (Plastics)

Synthetic materials with high performance.

  • Polyamides (Nylon/PA): High strength, used in gears, ski boots.
  • Polycarbonates (PC): High impact resistance. Used in helmets, optical discs.
  • ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene): Most common. Car bumpers, LEGO bricks.
  • PMMA (Acrylic): Transparent. Used in taillights.

B. Topology Optimization

Definition: A math method that optimizes material layout within a given space for a given set of loads.
Difference from Shape Optimization: Topology optimization can create holes and new shapes, not just resize existing ones.
Result: Organic, bone-like structures perfect for AM.

C. Advanced Topics

  • Bioprinting: Uses "Bioinks" (Cells + Hydrogels like Alginate/Collagen).
    Techniques: Extrusion-based or Droplet-based.
    Uses: Drug testing (reduce animal testing), Tissue Engineering.
  • Food Printing:
    Materials: Chocolate, dough, cheese, purees.
    Benefits: Customized nutrition for elderly/athletes.
  • Digital Assembly:
    Digital Instructions: Screens instead of paper (fewer errors).
    Digital Planning: Simulating the line before building it.
